In the previous section you just learnt that compounds which have a single bond between them are called saturated bonds. Among saturated bonds, those which have only hydrogen and carbon atoms are called alkanes.

Unsaturated hydrocarbons which have one more more double bonds are called alkenes and those with one or more triple bonds are called alkynes.

Compounds with only hydrogen and carbon are known as hydrocarbons. However carbon can also form bonds with other elements like oxygen, nitrogen and sulphur.

In any carbon compound, the element that can replace hydrogen is called a heteroatom.

The heteroatoms and the group containing these atoms are known as functional groups.
