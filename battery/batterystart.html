Negative zinc electrode Reacts with hydroxide molecules to form zinc hydroxide and release electrons in the process.

Manganese oxide positive electrode Manganese atom reacts with a water molecule (from the electrolyte) and releases hydroxide ions.

Separator sprayed with Potassium hydroxide electrolyte (KOH) The seperator stops the cathode and the anode from touching each other

Current collector- usually made from brass Collects electrons


The following reaction takes place in the battery :

Oxidation

Zn + 2OH- = ZnO + H2O + 2e-

Reduction

2MnO2 + H 2O + H2O + 2e- = Mn2O3 + 2OH-

Due to this reaction there is a build up of electrons in the current collector.

When you connect the battery to an electrical appliance, the electrons travel through the appliance's circuit and reach the positive terminal of the battery.
