Trends in the Periodic table

In ninth grade you learnt that elements are arranged in the periodic table according to their chemical properties.

In this section you will learn a a bit more about what these properties are.

Rows in the periodic table are called periods

Across a period from left to right the electronegativityThey form bonds by gaining electrons of atoms increases.

From left to right across a period, atoms have a greater nuclear charge and a smaller atomic sizeDistance between the center of the nucleus and the outermost shell of an atom. This combination allows the nucleus to attract the electrons strongly.

Columns in the periodic table are called groups.Elements in the same group have similar reactivity as they have a common number of electrons in their outermost shell.

Moving down a group, the electronegativity of atoms decreases.

Moving down a group, atoms increase in size as they have a greater number of energy shells.

Due to the extra energy levels and largeer atomic radius, elements further down in groups have less attraction for the bonding electrons.
