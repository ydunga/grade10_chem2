Types of chemical reactions
Combination Reaction

A combination reaction is a reaction where two or more elements combine to form a single compound.

Combination reactions are usually exothermicReactions that release energy, usually heat or light.

Below is an example of a combination reaction. Magnesium Oxide combines with Carbon Oxide to form Magnesium Carbonate

Even something as simple as burning of coal is an example of a combination reaction. When coal (which is carbon) combines with oxygen in the air, it produces heat and carbon dioxide.

Have you heard the saying that food is like fuel for your body? That's because during digestion, the food we eat is broken down into glucose. The glucose then combines with the oxygen in our body and releases energy. This process is known as respiration.

Decomposition reaction

A decomposition reaction is a reaction where a single reactant breaks down into simpler products.

Decomposition reactions often require energy - heat, light or electrcity to break down the reactants. Such reactions, where energy is absorbed during the chemical process are known as endothermic reactions.
