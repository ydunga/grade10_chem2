Displacement reaction

Before we talk about displacement reactions, let's have a look at the reactivity series.

The reactivity series places the most reactive element at the top. By reactive we mean elements which have a greater tendency to lose electrons and form positive ions.

Here you seee a reactivity series, which includes metals and non-metals.

A more reactive metal will displace a less reactive metal in a compound.

For example, magnesium is more reactive than copper and will displace Copper from the Copper Sulfate solution in the chemical reaction shown below.

This is an example of a displacement reaction.

A double displacement reaction is a reaction where two reactants exchange ions to form two new compounds.

A double displacement reaction will usually generate a precipitate.
