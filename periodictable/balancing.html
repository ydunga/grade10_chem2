Balancing chemical equations

Now that you know what the properties of different elements in the periodic table are, you can study how they combine with each other.

In the previous section we depicted a chemical reaction using a chemical formula.

The equation above is a balanced chemical equation.

If you recall from your 9th grade chemistry a law called - law of conservation of mass, then you will know that atoms cannot be created or destroyed in a chemical process. So the number of atoms of each element on each side of the equation (after and before the chemical process) have to balance.

The (s) (l) (aq) and (g) show you the state of each element. In our equation above, before the chemical reaction Potassium was in solid state and after the reaction it formed an aqueous solution of Potassium Hydroxide.

The hydrogen atoms in liquid water turn into gaseous state hydrogen during the chemical process.

Let's try balancing a chemical equation!

In order to balance the equation,first we have to find out how many atoms of each element are on either side of the equation.
