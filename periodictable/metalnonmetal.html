Metals

In metallic elements, the electrons leave the outermost shell and instead form a sea of 'delocalised' electrons which are free to move throughout the metallic substance. This property of metallic elements is what makes them good conductors of electricity.

Metals tend to lose electrons while forming bonds, i.e they are electropositive in nature.



Non-metals

Within the non-metallic elements, Nitrogen, Oxygen, Flourine, Phosphorus, Sulfur and Chlorine exist as discrete covalent molecules.

While others such as Boron, carbon and Silicon form large covalent networks.

These non-metallic elements have high melting points.

Non-metallic elements are electronegative i.e they form bonds by gaining electrons.
