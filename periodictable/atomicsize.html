As you move from left to right across a period, the atomic radius decreases. This is because the nucelar charge (number of protons) is also increasing as we move from left to right.

Due to the stronger nuclear charge, the electrons are pulled closer to the nucleus, which reduces the size of the atom.

As we move down a group, the atomic size increases.

This is because as we move down a group, additional shells are being added to the atom.

With each additional shell, the distance between the outermost electrons and the nucleus increases.
