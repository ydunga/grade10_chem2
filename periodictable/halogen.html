Halogens

The halogen group can be found on the right hand, non-metallic side of the periodic table.

F
Cl
Br
I
Ts

Halogens exist as simple molecules- where each molecule is made is made of a pair of atoms (diatomic) joined by a covalent bond.

Elements in the halogen group have seven electrons in their outer shell and are highly electronegative. In fact, flourine has the highest electronegativity of all elements.

Flourine is so reactive that it reacts with inert materials such as glass!



Reaction with metals

Halogens react with metals to form salts



Reaction with non metals

Halogens react with non-metals such as hydrogen to form hydrogen haldide.

The reactivity of halogens decreases as you move down the group.
