Group 1 elements have a valence of 1, i.e they have one electron in their outer shell.

Due to their electronic structure, elements in group will lose an electron and form an anion.

Such a reaction - where an electron is lost is known as oxidationLoss of electrons by a substance during a chemical reaction



Reaction with water

Alkali metals react vigorously with water. In this reaction hydrogen gas is released and a metal hydroxide (OH) is produced.

The (s) (aq) and (g) in these equation represent the state of the elements. The alkali elements are in solid state before the cheical reaction and in aqueous state after. Hydrogen is released in gaseous state during this process.

Reaction with Chlorine

Alkali metals react with Chlorine to form chlorides.
